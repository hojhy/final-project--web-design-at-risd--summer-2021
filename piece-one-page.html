Boiling A Frog


Risk is often associated with danger - the worst case scenario. But risk is not a risk if there wouldn't also be the positive outcome - something that is 'worth the risk'.

Countless scenarios in which things could fall apart play and entangle in my head with almost every decision I make. I've been let down a bit too many times (as we all have) and at some point, I decided being prepared for the disappointment and the disaster that could unfold were much more valuable than getting excited for what good could come out of it.

But all the risks I've taken and all the disappointing outcomes, not that I look back at it with a little more distance, had all led me to where I am now. And I kinda like where and who I am so all the risks I took - in the end - were all worth it.

Don't get tangled up in the paralysis of the worst case scenarios - even if the outcome disppoints you now, I promise it won't lead oyu astray. Not taking the risk - you just stand still. If the reason why you want to take the risk makes you thrilled with excitement and hope - you owe it to the possibility. Like why not?

(Also if you're happy where you are, you wouldn't even want to take the irsk at the first place. You're unhappy anyways just do it).
